Whats my solution to this high tax?

     

States Without High Tax

     To address the issue of high taxes in New York, a proposed solution involves a three-pronged approach: reducing funding from public services, optimizing tax credits and deductions, and implementing a flat income tax. The positive aspects of this solution include a more favorable tax rate for residents, simplifying the income tax structure, and potentially increasing incentives for work. On the flip side, potential negatives include heightened income inequality due to a flat tax system, and reduced funds available for crucial government services. This approach could impact various groups, affecting both the general population through altered tax burdens and potential service cutbacks, as well as those relying on public services that might experience reduced funding. The trade-offs between simplicity and potential inequalities must be carefully weighed in considering such a tax reform.

    Positives:

  • Better Tax Rate
  • Flat Income Tax being Simple
  • Increases Incentives for Work

    • Negatives:

  • Income Inequality
  • Reduced money for the government
